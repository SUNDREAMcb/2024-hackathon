When I moved to Austin in 2018, I swore to make my mark. I was a musician who barely practiced, but had a vision of creating. My first W2 job was slinging pizzas at Costco, but I was driven to create art in some way - My friend recruited me with their drag show talent agency Sister Fisters. I was hired to make video flyers to promote gigs for shows with a theme, some of my favorites involving Death Proof, and Rocky Horror Picture Show. I’d take clips from youtube to get the footage we were looking for, and overlay custom song mixes over top. This makes the work fall under Fair use, since we were transforming the work beyond its parts. All done in Windows Movie Maker (2007) and then exported to OneShot, a free, open-source video workstation that gave me a bit more control on effects. In 2019, I followed a Craigslist ad searching for ‘Strokes Cover band wanted’, where I met my would-be bandmates for the first time. From our first practice, we knew we were willing to put in the work, despite still needing a day job. I knew we had ‘made it’ when we announced our first big gig - Empire Control Room. Thanks to the support of the largest cover band in the world, The Red Not Chili Peppers, This is It was able to play to a huge crowd and get our name on Red River. Soon we’d find ourselves playing all the good venues, Stubb’s, Antone’s, Spider House, and The Parish. Since starting that band, I had gone from standing guard on a graveyard shift at IBC plaza, to managing tickets for WhatsApp, to optimizing SEO builds at TopSpot. We even played our biggest show to date the week before Austin shut down for the pandemic. I felt a taste of success, but not fulfillment, so I turned my eyes to previous experiences that interested me. Topspot opened my eyes to the search algorithm and how we can influence it. This goes hand in hand with b2b marketing as well as service as a sale. When working with Fortune 500 companies, the seconds that drag can cost millions. Moving quickly and with a strategy was the mindset, meaning Every action has to mean something and be backed by data. Now I see the implication for optimization. Conveying the most important data the quickest.
